## What This Is

Nepal's national economic data tells one story. What matters for job creation
and stopping outmigration is what happens at the **district level** — which
crops have export potential, which cooperatives are inactive, where hydropower
money should flow, and which markets are ready for local production.

This research maps all **77 districts** and **165 constituencies** with:

- 📊 **Income sources** — agriculture, crafts, remittances, tourism, hydropower
- 🏗 **Infrastructure gaps** — cold chain, processing, roads, cooperatives
- 🎯 **Priority interventions** — what to build, when, and at what priority
- 🌏 **Market opportunities** — domestic + export markets with real pricing data
- 👷 **Employment potential** — 6-year job creation outlook per district
- 💰 **Financing sources** — ADB, World Bank, federal grants, BOOT funds, NIFRA

|---|---|---|

| GDP per capita | USD 1,517 | Steady improvement |
| Remittances (% of GDP) | **33%** | 🔴 Highest on record |
| Electricity capacity | 3,602 MW | ⚡ Fastest-growing sector (21%) |
| Workers going abroad/year | 839,000+ | ↑ 28.6% annual growth |
| Public debt | 43.8% of GDP | Manageable but rising |
| Poverty rate | 20.3% | ↓ from 25.2% in 2011 |

> 🔑 **Core challenge:** Nepal's outmigration is structural. Remittances
> stabilise households but drain skilled workers. This research identifies
> what it takes to build quality jobs inside Nepal — one district at a time.
